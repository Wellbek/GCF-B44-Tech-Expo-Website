A.i Governance & Regional Enablement

A.i in Service of Climate Finance:

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how international institutions deliver on their mandates. At the Green Climate Fund (GCF), A.i is being introduced deliberately and responsibly to support the Fund's core mission: accelerating climate finance for developing countries with integrity, accountability, and transparency.

GCF's fundamental premise is straightforward: A.i augments what GCF's people do. It does not replace their judgment. Every A.i application at GCF operates with human-in-the-loop oversight, and no A.i system operates autonomously on matters that affect funding decisions, policy, or partner engagement. A.i tools are designed to serve all of GCF's partners equitably, with particular attention to the needs of least developed countries, small island developing states, and African states.

GCF's approach to A.i is grounded in a set of working principles for responsible A.i, a formal governance framework under development, and alignment with international guidance including the UN General Assembly resolution on safe, secure, and trustworthy A.i and the UNFCCC Technology Executive Committee's work on A.i and climate action.

Responsible Usage

Regional Presence

The Green Climate Fund (GCF) is establishing a regional presence to strengthen its ability to deliver impact by being closer to developing country partners and stakeholders, while maintaining strong institutional governance and oversight.

At its forty-first meeting, the GCF Board decided to establish regional offices to increase the efficiency and effectiveness of Secretariat operations, including programming and delivery support, in a manner that is cost-effective, operationally feasible, and aligned with GCF's governing framework.

Service Desk A.i Assistant (Pilot)

To support regional presence through technology, GCF is piloting the ServiceDesk A.I Agent.

The agent provides always-available, first-level IT support through a central communication natural language interface, helping staff get quick answers to common questions regardless of their location or time zone. By reducing reliance on location-specific support hours, it enables a more balanced and reliable support experience across headquarters and regional offices.

At the same time, all requests are captured and managed through a standardized service management approach, ensuring that incidents are consistently handled, tracked, and routed. This helps maintain a uniform level of service quality while improving visibility across regions.

The model combines automation with human oversight. Routine queries can be resolved quickly through guided support, while more complex or high-impact issues are escalated to IT personnel, ensuring accountability and quality where it matters most.

By enabling equitable access, standardized processes, and scalable support, the ServiceDesk A.I Agent contributes to GCF's goal of building a more integrated and operationally consistent regional footprint.

Efficient GCF

A.i Across the Project Lifecycle: GCF manages a project lifecycle that spans ten distinct stages, from country engagement and accreditation through to evaluation and project closure. A.i initiatives focus on areas where they can deliver the most immediate and measurable value, while building a foundation for responsible extension across the full lifecycle over time.

Introducing CLIMA: CLIMA — Collaborative Leading Intelligent Multi-purpose Assistant — is the name given to GCF's A.i assistant platform. It is no coincidence that "clima" means weather in Spanish and other languages. CLIMA represents GCF's commitment to ensuring that the tools it builds are as connected to its climate mandate as the work they support.

CLIMA brings together several A.i capabilities under a single, accessible identity, designed to guide GCF's partners through the Fund's processes: responding to questions, assisting with submission preparation, and supporting internal teams with complementary information during the review and appraisal process.

CLIMA SUITE 01
Status: Entering implementation — April 2026

CLIMA A.i Assistant

Conversational guidance across the entire project lifecycle.

The CLIMA A.i Assistant is a conversational interface that will be available through the GCF Partner Portal. Partners will be able to ask questions about accreditation, readiness, the Project Preparation Facility, concept notes, and funding proposal processes, and receive clear, accurate guidance in real time. This is especially valuable for entities engaging with GCF for the first time, or those with limited institutional support.

The assistant draws on GCF's official guidance and policy documents to ensure accuracy. Its performance is continuously monitored and refined based on the quality and accuracy of its responses. Partners are always encouraged to contact GCF directly for matters requiring case-specific guidance.

CLIMA SUITE 02
Status: Under development — Target production Q3 2026

CLIMA Smart Submissions

Intelligent document parsing for the origination and reporting stages.

CLIMA Smart Submissions allows partners to convert offline documents into structured digital submissions within the GCF Partner Portal. Rather than re-entering information that already exists in prepared documents, partners can upload their materials and the system extracts relevant data into the appropriate fields. This reduces duplication of effort and helps ensure data consistency across the submission and reporting cycle.

The tool is designed to accommodate the variety of formats GCF's partners use. Language support is an area under active development, reflecting the multilingual reality of GCF's partnership base.

CLIMA SUITE 03
Status: Under development — Target production Q3 2026

CLIMA Researcher

A.i-supported research and analysis for project development and review.

The CLIMA Researcher supports project teams by generating complementarity and coherence analyses, as well as country context summaries. This provides review teams with a structured starting point for their assessment, grounded in available data, and helps ensure that the information considered during project development and concept review is comprehensive and current.

CLIMA SUITE 04
Status: Under development — Target production Q3 2026

CLIMA A.i-Assisted Appraisal

Structured screening and review support at the appraisal stage.

GCF has developed A.i-assisted screening and review capabilities to support the funding proposal appraisal process. These include tools for locally led climate action (LLCA) screening, which estimate the degree of local government ownership and community engagement within a given project, and structured review toolkits for the simplified approval process that help reviewers work through proposals more consistently and efficiently.

A.i-assisted screening at this stage automates a checklist-based review and flags areas that warrant closer attention from human reviewers. This does not replace the review itself. It ensures that reviewers can direct their expertise where it matters most, while maintaining a consistent, criteria-based approach across all submissions.

CLIMA SUITE 05
Status: Under development — Target production Q3 2026

CLIMA Impact Synthesizer

Lessons learned synthesis and institutional knowledge retrieval.

The CLIMA Impact Synthesizer supports the development of a centralized lessons learned capability that captures insights from completed and ongoing projects. A.i powers the synthesis and retrieval of institutional knowledge, enabling project teams to draw on past experience when appraising new proposals.

As GCF's portfolio grows, the volume of accumulated experience becomes an increasingly valuable asset, but only if it is organized and accessible. The CLIMA Impact Synthesizer helps bridge that gap, turning lessons from individual projects into practical guidance for future ones. For example, when appraising a coastal resilience project, teams could surface lessons from similar projects in comparable contexts, ensuring that institutional knowledge informs new decisions.

Status: Concept stage

A.i-Assisted Legal Arrangements (Concept)

Exploratory initiative for structured drafting support.

GCF is at an early stage of exploring whether A.i can assist with generating initial drafts of Funded Activity Agreements for approved funding proposals. This would involve producing a structured first draft based on the approved proposal, which legal and operational staff would then review and finalize.

This initiative is currently a concept under evaluation. Any development would proceed within the governance and oversight frameworks that guide all A.i use at GCF. No A.i-generated legal document would proceed without thorough human review.

Looking Ahead

GCF is focusing first on areas that deliver immediate operational value: reducing processing time, improving consistency, and giving partners better support throughout their engagement with the Fund.

As confidence and capacity grow, GCF will progressively extend A.i capabilities across the project lifecycle. Each extension will be guided by evidence from real-world use and feedback from partners and staff. The Fund iterates, refines, and scales responsibly — piloting, evaluating, and expanding based on demonstrated results.

As these tools mature, GCF is open to exploring how the approaches and lessons it develops could benefit its broader partnership, consistent with GCF's role in supporting developing country capacity.

Digital Innovations

Multilingualism

In line with its regional presence, multilingual and multicultural engagement bears a high significance. Modern technology, including A.I, is recognized as a valuable complement in such an environment, enhancing accessibility and efficiency while supporting established human interpretation practices.

GCF is exploring live translation technology with measured optimism, recognizing that accuracy and contextual nuance are critical in a multilateral setting. The current approach includes internal trials combined with validation by expert translators and interpreters, a clear emphasis on augmentation rather than replacement, and ongoing engagement with leading technology vendors, including Microsoft.

Examples of such technologies were featured at the GCF Board 44 Technology Exhibition in Songdo, including:

  • •
    Multilingual Presentations: Microsoft Teams enables participants to view presentation slides in their preferred language in real time without affecting others. It allows real-time slide translation into multiple languages.
  • •
    Multilingual Live Captions: Teams can provide real-time transcription and translation of spoken language during meetings. It supports a wide range of spoken languages with translation into multiple target languages, including UN languages, and offers both A.i-generated and human captioning options. Caption data is not retained after meetings and is processed within the organization's geographical region.
  • •
    Teams Live Interpreter: Microsoft Teams Live Interpreter enables real-time spoken-language interpretation within Teams meetings, allowing a human or A.i interpreter to provide simultaneous interpretation that attendees can select as an audio channel — closely mirroring the experience of traditional conference interpretations.
  • •
    Microsoft Copilot Vision Mode: Allows users to share their screen and engage in a natural-language conversation with A.i about the content being viewed, including documents, presentations, and across various languages and content types.

The Frontier Firm
